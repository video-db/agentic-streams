### Source proof

- Source file: `2026-04-01/research/adp-official.md`
- Key line: "Private sector employment increased by 62,000 jobs in March"

**Secondary reporting:** CNBC
- Source file: `2026-04-01/research/adp-cnbc.md`

### Timing

### Market evidence
- S&P 500 opened near **6575.34** and was around **6600.01** by 12:28 PM ET
- Nasdaq was up **0.60%** from open by the same time
- 10Y yield was slightly lower intraday despite the better-than-expected jobs print

### Interpretation
This was **not** a classic “hot jobs report -> yields spike -> stocks sell off” reaction. The better-than-feared number appeared to support risk sentiment, but the report itself also showed hiring concentrated in health care and construction rather than broad labor-market strength.

### Confidence
**Tier B — reasonable evidence**

- The market reaction was positive
- But the move cannot be attributed to ADP alone because other same-day forces were also in play
